## Overview

Largemouth bass (*Micropterus salmoides*) are the flagship warmwater fly rod species. They're aggressive, forgiving of imperfect presentations, and found in an enormous range of water from small farm ponds to major river backwaters.

## Habitat & Behavior

Largemouth are ambush predators. They hold in cover — laydowns, lily pad edges, dock pilings, undercut banks — and strike opportunistically rather than actively pursuing prey across open water. Look for:

- **Shallow flats with structure** in early morning and evening
- **Deep shade** near banks and docks midday in summer
- **Vegetation edges** (hydrilla, coontail, milfoil) year-round
- **Points and transitions** where hard bottom meets soft

## Seasonal Patterns

| Season | Behavior | Target Areas |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Spring (pre-spawn) | Actively feeding, aggressive | Shallow flats warming in sun |
| Spawn | Territorial, guarding nests | Visible beds in 1–4 ft |
| Summer | Heat-stressed, dawn/dusk bite | Deep shade, cover, early/late |
| Fall | Feeding heavily pre-winter | Follows baitfish to shallower water |
| Winter | Sluggish, slow presentation needed | Deep, stable-temp water |

## Fly Selection

**Topwater (most fun)**
- Poppers and sliders: early morning, evening, overcast days
- Deer hair bugs (Whitlock's Bass Bug, Dahlberg Diver)
- Size 1/0–2/0

**Sub-surface**
- Clousers in chartreuse/white or olive/white
- Crawfish patterns near rocky/gravelly banks
- Large streamers (Game Changer, Drunk and Disorderly)

**Weedless rigs** are essential in vegetation — use a stiff monofilament weed guard or bend the hook slightly.

## Tackle

- **Rod:** 7–9 wt, 9 ft. A 7 wt covers most panfish-to-bass overlap; go 9 wt for big bugs and wind.
- **Line:** WF floating with a short, aggressive taper for turning over bulky flies
- **Leader:** 7.5–9 ft, 10–16 lb tippet. Keep it simple — bass aren't leader-shy.

## Notes

Bass are far more forgiving of presentation than trout. Slap the bug on the water near cover, let it sit, then twitch. Many strikes come on the pause or the first twitch after the fly lands.

In the Illinois backwaters, largemouth stack up near wingdams and in the slack water behind them in summer. The Atchafalaya produces big fish around cypress knees in April and May.
