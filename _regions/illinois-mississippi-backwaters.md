## Overview

The upper Mississippi River backwater system near Granite City, IL is one of the Midwest's most underutilized warmwater fisheries. The river's wing dams, chutes, side channels, and oxbow sloughs hold substantial populations of largemouth and smallmouth bass, bluegill, crappie, and carp — all of which can be taken on the fly.

## Access Points

- **Calhoun Point / Brussels Ferry area** — Remote sloughs on the Illinois side; accessible by boat
- **Alton Pool (Pool 26)** — From the Alton Marina or ramps near Grafton; good structure fishing
- **Piasa Creek confluence** — Wade-accessible for early-season smallmouth
- **Horseshoe Lake (Madison County)** — Oxbow with heavy panfish and bass; shore and float tube access

## Target Species

| Species | Best Months | Primary Habitat |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Largemouth Bass | Apr–Oct | Slough edges, timber, vegetation |
| Smallmouth Bass | Apr–Jun, Sep–Oct | Rocky wingdams, main channel |
| Bluegill / Sunfish | May–Aug | Spawning flats, dock structure |
| Carp | May–Sep | Flats, shallow backwaters |
| Crappie | Mar–May | Flooded timber, dock pilings |

## Seasonal Notes

**Spring (March–May):** Prime time. Water warming, fish shallow and active. Bluegill spawn in May on visible beds. Crappie stack in timber. Bass pre-spawn feeding is aggressive.

**Summer (June–August):** Fish early and late. Midday heat pushes activity deep. Topwater from first light until 9 AM; return at dusk. The sloughs stay productive longer than main channel.

**Fall (September–October):** Underrated. Baitfish schooling, bass following. Clouser/streamer fishing productive. Weather windows can produce all-day bites.

**Winter:** Largely catch-and-release only; fish deeply lethargic.

## Fly Patterns That Work Here

- **Poppers (white, chartreuse, black)** — Early/late for bass and bluegill
- **Clousers (chartreuse/white, olive/white)** — Workhorse for bass and crappie
- **Wooly Buggers (black, olive, brown)** — Versatile; cast to timber and strip
- **Spawning Bluegill pattern** — For carp on the flats in spring
- **Small soft hackles / nymphs** — Crappie in timber

## Logistics & Notes

A canoe, kayak, or small jon boat opens up most of the best water. The sloughs accessible only by boat see far less pressure. Water clarity varies — after rain events the main channel runs muddy; the backwater sloughs clear faster and hold fish better in those conditions.

Be aware of river stage. High water floods access ramps and makes the backwaters fishable but difficult to navigate; low water concentrates fish but some sloughs become too shallow.

> **Local tip:** The section of slough immediately behind Calhoun County's flooded timber stands holds big bluegill all summer. Best on a small popper or hard-hackle wet fly, size 10–12.
